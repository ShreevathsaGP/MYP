INTRODUCTION

What is the E-Portfolio?

All Block 8 subjects (including design, art, music and PHE) and Second languages do not have an E-assessment. Instead, they are graded through what is known as the E-portfolio. The E-portfolio is a similar project to the MYP Personal Project. The E-portfolio is very different for all the aforementioned subjects.

What do you have to do in the E-Portfolio?

What you have to do in the e-portfolio changes from subject to subject. In the case of the design eportfolio, students have to use their technical skills and knowledge to solve a contemporary problem of concern. For the arts eportfolio, students have to use their knowledge and skills in art or music to create a create and unique composition which depicts the IB declared theme/global context. For the second langugaes eportfolio, students have to complete four in-school tasks which revolve around a common topic.

DESIGN E-PORTFOLIO

BRIEF

  • A project which expects digital design students to use their technical abilities and knowledge to solve a contemporary topic of concern.
  • Students may use various means to solve this problem, such as a program, a short film, a game or any other means.
  • There are four criteria on the basis of which this project would be graded.
  • The student is expected to make a working product, or appropriate evidence for it, and write a report in 40 pages.
  • The report is evidence showing the journey the student embarked while tackling and solving the problem and making their final product.

    • APPENDIX

    What do you indlude in the appendix?

    • Any code, images, or non-essential elements to the report that are used to substantiate claims made in the report, and do not make any statements in themselves
    • Any charts, like Know Want Learn charts, that would otherwise take too much space on the 40 page limit report.
    • Evidence of outcome, development and planning.
    • What you attach in the Appendix will not be graded, so be mindful with the fact that they are there to substantiate any claims you made in the actual report, and do not have any integrity by themselves.

    SUBMISSION

    How do you submit the eportfolio?

    • You first submit your report itself (40 page limit), through an online or written medium.
    • You then attach an appendix to the submission, which contains all non-essential elements (will not be graded).
    • You then have a max 4 minute video, physical submission, (sometimes applicable picture submission) of your final product/solution.
    • These three must be seperated separately, and if incomplete will cause a huge loss in your grades.

    ARTS E-PORTFOLIO

    BRIEF

    • The eportfolio for all arts (including music and visual art) is completely different from the design eportfolio
    • Unlike the design eportfolio, where students had to utilise their technical skills and knowledge to solve a problem, in the arts eportfolio, students must create, present or perform art that displays a message under the IB declared theme.
    • Below is how the design cycle differs for art and music.

    MAKING THE REPORT

    1. Presentation (Criterion A and D)
      • An individual research presentation using concepts and subject-specific terminology of -->
        • Art that demonstrates the IB declared context/theme including research about the artist(s)
        • The critical analysis of one or two examples of the art form
    2. Development (Criterion B and C)
      • A selection of process journal extracts to show -->
        • The development of the student’s artistic intention
        • The exploration of artistic ideas
        • Skills and techniques acquired during the progression of the work must be shown at three stages: near the start, the middle and towards the end of the development process.
    3. Outcome (Criterion B)
      • Here is what the outcome entails:
        • The student’s artwork created, presented or performed that demonstrated the IB declared context/theme
    4. Commentary (Criterion D)
      • A commentary that includes:
        • Evidence of the student’s ability to construct meaning and transfer learning into their artwork
        • A critique of the student’s own artwork including its ability to demonstrate the IB declared context

    SECOND LANGUAGE E-PORTFOLIO

    What do you do in the second language eportfolio?

    Unlike the design and art eportfolios, the tasks for the second language eportfolios cannot be done at home. So they are done in school, under teacher supervision. Due to this, all the content for the eportfolio will be taught in class by your respective second language teacher(s)

    What are the tasks for the second language eportfolio?

    • As you all know, there are four criterion in second language, and they are -->

      1. Criterion A: Listening
        • As the name suggests, this is the criteria where students have to comprehend and answers questions based on an audio or video. These questions can be objective or subjective.
      2. Criterion B: Reading
        • This is the criteria where students have to comprehend passages and answer a series of questions. These questions can be objective or subjective.
      3. Criterion C: Speaking
        • This is where students are graded for their pronounciation and fluency while speaking the langugae
        • For the eportfolio, students will be provided with 50 guiding questions before hand, for which they can prepare answers at home and get them reviewed by their teacher
        • Students will be asked questions in a private, recorded, face-to-face oral session that revolve around the theme of the provided guiding questions
      4. Criterion D: Writing
        • This is the criteria where students are provided with a theme and a certain format (a blog, letter, poster, or any others) and the students must write a response in the chosen langugae
        • Here, students are graded on their grammar, word choice, and flow
    • The teachers will conduct as many tasks as they like for each criterion. While one teacher may conduct one task for each criterion, another might conduct three. This decision is made by the teacher. If multiple tasks are conducted for each criterion, then the best of the scores is considered.
    • The best way to preapre for the second language eportfolio, would be to pay attention and take diligent notes in your second language classes, and reviewing your previous years vocabularly.
