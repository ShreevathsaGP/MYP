INTRODUCTION

What is the IDU exam?
  1. IDU is short for interdisciplinary Understanding
  2. Is a unit where students understand events and concepts by combining two or more MYP subjects
  3. With this interdisciplinary understanding, students try to resolve contemporary problems that people face on a global level

QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

How do the IDU examinations work?
  • IDU is an e-assessment subject and the students will have to appear for an e-assessment like any other e-assessment subject
  • The examination is for two hours and for 80 marks
  • Students will be asked questions regarding a set of sources, which would have been provided in advance by the school

How to prepare for IDU examinations?
  • The school will provide students with a 'SOURCE BOOKLET' and focused disciplines/subjects, well in advance
  • The source booklet contains all the relevant sources which the exam will revolve around
  • Students must go through each and every source and annotate the sources with respect to the provided global context and focused disciplines
  • In addition to that, it is advised that students revise some of their subject knowledge with respect to the chosen subjects and global context
  • Students can also read articles and external information about the sources

CRITERIONS FOR EXAM

Criterion A: Disciplinary Grounding
  • This is the criteria where students have to use their subject specific knowledge and factual information to come to conclusions
  • Typically, questions pertaining only to criteria A will be only for 2 - 4 marks

Criterion B: Synthesising
  • This is the criteria where students have to combine knowledge and skills of the different subjects in order to propose a solution or infer something
  • The source booklet contains all the relevant sources which the exam will revolve around
  • These type of questions are typically long answers and require students to write essays
  • These questions range from 8 marks to 15 marks
  • A context might be provided to the students - such as a letter, article, speech etc. - based on which their answer's format must change

Criterion C: Communication
  • This is the criteria where students use appropriate language, linguistic devices and factual information in order to bring forth their message
  • Based on the context of the question, the requirements changes. For instance, the language, devices and information included in a speech is different from those included in a letter. Based on the context provided to the students, the requirements changes

Criterion D: Reflection
  • This is the criteria where students are meant to reflect on their aforementioned proposals and solutions and evalute them
  • The source booklet contains all the relevant sources which the exam will revolve around
  • They reflect on the sources and their own proposals and evaluate their strengths and limiations

Note :- Usually, criterion B, C and D are combined
