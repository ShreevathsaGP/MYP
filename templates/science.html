PHYSICS

INTRODUCTION

  • Physics is a subject, with minimal content. However, to score well in the exams you will need a very deep understanding of the concepts in addition to how those concepts reflect in day to day life.
  • The best resource for MYP Physics by far is the textbook provided by the IBO → https://www.amazon.in/MYP-Physics-Concept-Based-Approach/dp/0198375557 OR https://www.amazon.in/Physics-IB-MYP-Concept/dp/1471839338/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1&keywords=Myp+physics+textbook&qid=1588502579&s=books&sr=1-1
  • There are four criteria in physics and they are -->
    1. Criterion A: Knowing and Understanding
      • This is the criteria which has direct questions and tests the students on their factual knowledge
      • These questions are usually associated with the command terms state, explain, describe, list, calculate, find, and other similar terms
      • These questions range from 2 marks to 8 marks, depending on the command term used
    2. Criterion B: Inquiring and Designing
      • This is the criteria which requries students to use their knowledge in physics to create investigations or lab procedures
      • Students must mention the research question, aim, hypothesis, variables (independent, dependent and atleast 3 controlled variables), materials/equipment, procedure and a data table, in their answers. Based on the number of marks, command term used, and the bullet points provided, few of the above mentioned elements may be omitted.
      • These questions range from 6 marks to 12 marks
    3. Criterion C: Processing and Evaluating
      • This is the criteria where the student needs to derive conclusions from graphs, and/or evalute the validity of an experiment
      • Questions pertaining to this criteria could either be to describe the general trend in a graph, or explain why a certain graphical trend is taking place. It could also require students to plot a graph based on the calculated or provided data points. Few questions would be linked to either a procedure they have developed (in a previous question) or one that is provided and they have to evalute the method, suggest improvements or extensions to the experiment, and comment on the validity of the hypothesis.
    4. Criterion D: Reflecting on the Impact of Science
      • This is a lengthy criteria which requires students to do more typing and reading
      • Questions in this criteria are usually based on a provided text. Students have to read the text and analyse the social, economical, environmental, political, moral, and ethical factors that are affected by the problem/solution in the text. This analysis must be done whilst using scientific terminology.
      • The scientific terminology used is not only limited to physics. Terms from chemistry and biology can also be mentioned, as long they suffice the explaination for the above mentioned factors.

    The best way to prepare for all of these criterion would be to answer the questions provided in the MYP years 4 and 5 Physics textbook. The book has questions after each chapter, and the questions are a mix of all criteria. Answering those questions would be the best way to practice.

    SAMPLE QUESTIONS

    • A. The perfume contains complex mixtures of esters. The reaction of alcohol and carboxylic acid produces ester. This reaction is a reversible reaction and after a few days reaches equilibrium. Most reactions producing esters are exothermic.

      Equation: alcohol + carboxylic acid = ester + water

      Using the above equation, explain why open bottles of perfumes are ideally stored in cold and dry conditions.


      • By putting the perfume bottles in a cold and dry condition, the entire system (both reactants and products) are subjected to a strain or change. By putting the system in dry conditions, the amount of water is kept constant and there is no change. If there was a change in the amount of water, then the backward reaction will be favoured, the equilibrium would shift to the left, and would result in the products going back to form reactants. By putting the system in a cold condition, the amount of ester formed increases. This is because the forward reaction is an exothermic reaction and a reduction in temperature favours exothermic reaction. This causes the equilibrium to shift to the right, thus forming more ester.

      There are around 20,000 species of bees across the world that are found on every continent except Antarctica, bees depend upon pollen as a source of protein, they depend upon flower nectar as a source of energy.

      These are the most important pollinators of our fruits, vegetables, flowers and crops, more than 1/3 of the world’s population depends on pollination by bees.

Modern industrialization of farming has changed how farmers operate their farms, farmers have stopped planting crops such as clover and alfalfa which are natural fertilisers that fix nitrogen in the soil.
