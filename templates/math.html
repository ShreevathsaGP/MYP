MATHEMATICS

INTRODUCTION

  • Unlike the math done in other boards, MYP mathematics is an extremely conceptual and application based subject. Although there are a few direct questions, most of the questions are where the students have to utilise their mathematical knowledge and skills in a real life situation.
  • MYP math is offered in two levels, Regular and Enrichment.
  • The difference between the two, is that enrichment math has a few extra topics which regular math does not cover. In terms of the pace of the class, they are pretty much the same. Another difference, enrichment math questions are relatively harder, when compared to regular math questions.
  • Unfortunately there are not many resources online to practice math. The best way to practice MYP style questions, would be to do the questions provided by your teachers.
  • There are four criteria in math, and they are -->
    1. Criterion A: Knowing and Understanding
      • This is the criteria where students will be tested purely on their mathematical knowledge and skills
      • In this criteria, questions are usually direct, thus of less marks
      • The questions are usually linked with the command terms such as identify, calculate, Show that and other similar terms
    2. Criterion B: Inquiring and Designing
      • This is the criteria where students have to investigate using mathematical reasoning and knowledge and identify patterns in a real life scenario.
      • This criteria is usually paired with Criterion C: Communication, thus grading the students not only their mathematical reasoning and investigation, but also on the way they communicate their findings using appropriate calculations and justifications.
      • The questions in this criteria come with command terms such as write down, predict, determine, verify, and other similar terms.
    3. Criterion C: Processing and Evaluating
      • This criteria is usually paired along with the other criteria
      • This is the aspect which assesses students on their mathematical reasoning and justification and how appropriate they are to the specific scenario and question.
    4. Criterion D: Reflecting on the Impact of Science
      • This criteria, like criteria B, is where students are expected to use their mathematical reasoning and logic to solve real life problems or analyse contemporary problems.
      • This criteria too is paired with criterion C
      • Questions in this criteria are usually found with command terms like suggest, justify, determine, and other similar terms.

    The best way to prepare for all of this is to simply practice problems from all the topics mentioned below.

      3. n Length AQ
      1 3√5
      2 3√10
      3 3√17
      4 3√26
      5 3√37

      As we can see in the above table, the values of length AQ follow a pattern. The coefficient of the root stays constant at 3, however, the value under the root changes. From this we can understand that the pattern is in the values under the root. The values under the root seem to be increasing by a difference which is increasing by a common difference of 2.

      n Length AQ
      1 3√5
      2 3√10
      3 3√17
      4 3√26
      5 3√37

      Difference 1 = 5 , 7 , 9 , 11 ...

      Difference 2 = 2 , 2 , 2 ...

      2a = 2
      a = 1       		3a + b = 5
      3(1) + b= 5
      b = 2       		a + b + c = 5
      1 + 2+ c = 5
      c = 2

      ∴ Length AQ = 3√n^2 + 2n + 2

      To test the general rule, let us take the value n = 1. We know that when n = 1, the length AQ = 3√5

      ∴ Length AQ = 3√1^2 + 2(1) + 2

      = 3√1 + 2 + 2

      = 3√5

      We get the same value, hence the rule has been tested.

      To verify and justify the rule, let us take the value of n = 4. From the table we know that when n=4, AQ = 3√26

      ∴ Length AQ = 3√4^2 + 2(4) + 2

      = 3√16 + 8 + 2

      = 3√26

      ​Since we get the same value, the rule has been verified and proved to be valid.

    3. D. The maximum heart rate (H) in beats per minute (bpm) is calculated in terms of age, (a) in years using the formula H = 208 - 0.7a. A safe exercise is one that stays below the maximum heart rate.
      Show that the maximum heart rate of a 16 year old is 197 bpm to the nearest beat.



      4. ANSWER

      H = 208 - 0.7a

      = 208 - 0.7(16)

      = 208 - 11.2

      =196.8

      H = 197 bpm
